Nowadays there are too many minecraft servers out there, due to minecraft server hosts making it too easy for 12 year olds to take their parent's credit card and purchase their own server. Minecraft itself is also becoming saturated with 12 year olds. As of right now, there are 125,000+ minecraft servers, with a peak of around 400k players and at near 100k lowest. If all players were spread out evenly there would be only like 4 players per server, but we don't see that do we? The only popular servers are those massive ones with custom gamemodes, custom minigames, custom plugins, custom everything, making them 100%-ish unique. What do we have that others don't? Not much. Unless there's some unique and brilliant idea that can get us lotta players, we won't be that unique. However, I did have some server types that are pretty unique and shouldn't be too hard to create with scripting, but I don't have the time to work on anything nor the motivation to do so. Maybe during summer. Maybe not.

Nearly every minigame that is available for the public to download are incredibly overused, such as Survival Games, Mob Arena, etc., so minigames aren't going to be implemented anytime soon as they will just add more things to maintain, as well as spread players out over the server which lowers the interaction between players and community of the server.

The only place that actually brings players is Planetminecraft, which if you have not noticed already, brings quite a few of unwanted guests. Planetminecraft has a system where you can bump a server to the top of the list every 24 hours, and is one of the few server listings that has this feature. Other server lists only get new players to join within the hour you post the server, and only during that. The server post will just die along with the other thousands of dead postings. Most server lists are dominated by the servers that pay for a "sponsored" spot, which costs up to $800-$1,000. The voting section's first 30-50 pages have few hundred to several thousand votes, which we will never get close to. Realistically, people rarely even go past first few pages, so unless we magically get few thousand votes within a month, voting does not help at all.

Prison servers has been around for a few years already, and every prison server seemingly has the exact same layout, ranks A to something, then free. Prison servers don't exactly have much ways of being unique, which is the prime way of making players stay... unless someone has a really good idea that can be done within our ranges. Also, the extra prison server would mean more maintenance, needs more moderation, more work to do. One server is already more than enough for me to maintain, another one would require a staff member who actually knows how to operate and run a server successfully, not just in game moderation/administration.

Contests should help as it does give players something to do, but last time's contest didn't go so well. Around 4 people entered and there were 3 winners. Obviously having the near same amount of winners and people who enter isn't exactly very ideal, but contests will most likely still appear later on.

The points system would just be the economy(?). In order to keep it fair it would have to be reset every once in a while, as inflation does take it's toll on minecraft servers often. Many players don't like resets as it makes them lose all their progress and may chose to leave, just like how a specific server had reset it's map several times and now is nearly dead due to the players that had quit.
